Questions people ask

A few things worth knowing.

What does a first engagement look like?

No hard sell, no long proposal before you have seen any value. It starts with a conversation.

Week one: a focused Discovery conversation with you and whoever needs to be in the room. We learn how you are set up, where it hurts, and what good looks like. No pitch. Just the right questions.

Month one: the quick wins are in motion. Ownership is clear. Your vendors know who they are dealing with. You stop being the person in the middle. The first monthly update lands and it makes sense.

Ongoing: what clients say most often, around month three or four, is "I don't think about IT anymore." Not because nothing happens — because when it does, it gets handled.

Does BetaSys replace our existing IT team or MSP?

It depends on how you engage.

For Strategy & Governance and Professional Services engagements, BetaSys sits above or alongside what you already have. Your existing team or MSP keeps doing what they do — and BetaSys makes sure it is heading somewhere sensible, with real accountability.

For the full Technology Partner model, BetaSys becomes your technology function. That does not mean replacing people for the sake of it — but it does mean owning the outcome end-to-end: strategy, delivery, security and vendor management.

BetaSys is not a helpdesk. Not a ticket queue. It is the partner accountable for all of it.

How does pricing work?

Strategy & Governance is a fixed monthly retainer. Professional Services engagements are scoped and priced per project — clear scope agreed before any work begins. The full Technology Partner model is one monthly fee covering strategy, governance, security, vendor management and delivery.

No additional labour charges on top of agreed scope. For large projects or on-site work outside Auckland, the approach is agreed upfront.

No surprises.

What size of organisation does BetaSys work with?

If you are big enough to feel real pain from technology or security that does not quite work — but not large enough to justify a full-time CIO, CISO and internal IT team — you are likely a good fit.

BetaSys typically works with New Zealand businesses in manufacturing, logistics, primary industry, construction and professional services — often multi-site, often with both IT and OT environments, and usually spending meaningful money on technology without a senior person governing how it is used.

Some clients start with a single assessment to understand where they stand. Others come straight to an ongoing engagement. Both work.

Do you work with boards and executives directly?

Yes — and it matters.

Many organisations engaging BetaSys have a board or CEO asking harder questions about technology risk, cyber insurance requirements, or why projects keep stalling. Part of the value is translating the technology picture into language that works at board level: clear risk posture, honest progress, and a defensible investment story.

If you need someone who can sit in a board meeting and hold the technology and security conversation — that is a core part of what BetaSys offers.

What experience does BetaSys have in OT and industrial environments?

Significant hands-on experience across New Zealand manufacturing, food processing, primary industry and logistics environments — including settings where corporate IT systems meet operational technology, SCADA, PLCs and field assets.

OT environments carry different risk profiles to traditional IT: safety, uptime and legacy technology all intersect. BetaSys brings both the IT governance lens and the OT operational reality together, rather than applying a standard IT framework to an environment it does not fit.

What if we need on-site support outside Auckland?

BetaSys has a long-standing network of trusted partners across New Zealand, including in manufacturing and primary industry environments where on-site presence matters.

For work requiring hands-on delivery outside Auckland, BetaSys brings in people it knows well and has worked with before. You deal with BetaSys. BetaSys deals with them. The accountability stays in one place.
